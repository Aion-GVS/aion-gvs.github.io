The Problem

Institutions currently report their own compliance.

Across every sector — public, private, regulatory — the dominant model of governance verification is self-attestation. Institutions declare their own compliance. Auditors review documents. Reports are filed. Trust is assumed.

There is no system-level mechanism to independently verify that governance states are real, current, or structurally sound — without relying on the institution being governed. That is the gap GVS is designed to close.

The Framework

Governance Verification System

GVS is a system-level framework for verifying governance states, compliance structures, and institutional actions — independently of trust-based reporting. It replaces policy statements with proof and declarations with verification.

GVS operates as an independent verification layer: cryptographically anchored, auditable, and structurally agnostic to the institution it governs. It does not ask institutions to self-report. It verifies.

Standards Development Pathway

GVS has been submitted to the Digital Governance Standards Institute (DGSI) — a Standards Council of Canada accredited Standards Development Organization — for consideration as a National Standard of Canada. The 60-day public evaluation period is a formal component of that process.

About GVS

A Canadian-sovereign governance standard.

The Governance Verification System was developed by a Canadian inventor and entrepreneur in response to a systemic gap in how institutions prove — rather than merely declare — their compliance and governance states.

GVS was submitted independently to the national standards process. The framework is protected under Canadian sovereign IP provisions and is not available for foreign licensing prior to formal ratification as a National Standard of Canada.

The mission of GVS is to make governance verifiable — at the system level, independent of institutional interest, and provable by design.
